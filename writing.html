Writing Experience

As the Intern to the Office of the President at Tufts University, I've had the pleasure of drafting a number of speeches and communications for the university's 11th president, Anthony Monaco. Of particular note are the 2013 Matriculation Address, delivered to over 2000 students, parents, faculty, and staff, and a number of comprehensive reports to the Board of Trustees.

In 2010, I wrote and produced "The Border: Arizona, SB1070, and the Immigration Question," a feature-length documentary exploring the multi-faceted issues of illegal immigration and immigration policy in the state of Arizona. "The Border" debuted on May 6th, 2011 at the Phoenix International Film Festival as a showcase feature, then went on to enjoy a brief theatrical run in theaters across the state. "The Border" received numerous awards and recognition from C-SPAN and the Student Television Network as an outstanding student documentary. Watch the trailer for "The Border" here, or check out the work of some of the other members of the team: Yihyun Jeong, Myles Kramer, Danny Miller.

As an undergraduate, I've produced research papers, position papers, memos, and literary critiques in pursuit of my diploma. My curriculum has focused heavily on political science and english courses (in addition to my concentration in computer science), but covers a swath of other disciplines including Philosophy, Drama, Film Production, and soon History and Psychology. Samples of my work are available upon request.

My journalism experience began accumulating in high school, where through my school's media program I was able to acquire professional written and broadcast journalism skills. In both 2010 and 2011 I led the program's news production team to second place in the nation for on-site broadcast production at the Student Television Network conference. I also produced and directed a teen news show and "Student Conversations", an interview program for Scottsdale Public Television featuring guests including former Supreme Court Justice Sandra Day O'Connor, Sheriff Joe Arpaio, and E Street Band guitarist Nils Lofgren. More recently, I founded the news division of Tufts Television, our student-run production house.
